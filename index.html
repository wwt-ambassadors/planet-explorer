Our solar system is located in the Milky Way, one of many galaxies in the universe. The solar system formed from a cloud of gas and dust more than 4.5 billion years ago. All the planets orbit the Sun in the same direction along elliptical, or oval, paths and within a similar plane. The planets in the inner solar system are relatively close together and mostly composed of rock. The planets in the outer solar system are far apart and mostly composed of gases and icy fluids.

TIPS

How to zoom & pan

  • To zoom, scroll in or out (or use the Z-X keys).
  • To pan around, click and drag (or use the I-J-K-L keys).

Don’t see anything?

  • Sometimes you’re facing the nighttime side of a planet. Try panning around the object to get to the daytime side.
  • Sometimes the image data loads slowly - if you’re sure you’re not on the nighttime side, wait a few moments for the object imagery to load.

Object size

  • By default, objects in the Solar System are displayed 10x larger than their actual size (i.e. NOT to scale).
  • The more you move the slider to the left, the more exaggerated the object sizes will be (and the further they will be from their actual scale).

Time controls

  • By default, time runs at actual speed. (If you watch Earth for 24 hours, it would spin around once).
  • Speed time up a little to watch most planets and moons spin on their axes and experience day and night.
  • Speed time up a lot to watch planets orbit around the Sun and experience a year (or to watch moons orbit their planets).

Realistic lighting

  • Leave “on” if you want to observe day/night on planets and moons.
  • Turn “off” if you want to look for features on planets and moons that are on the dark nighttime sides.
